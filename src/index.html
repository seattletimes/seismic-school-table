On shaky ground

Search below for data on every public-school building in the state, provided by the Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction. The data include: a) an estimate of how hard the ground can shake called peak ground acceleration, or PGA, expressed as a fraction of gravity's acceleration; b) the type of ground a building rests on; and c) the year each building was built. These data offer only a broad view of earthquake hazards that schools face and do not indicate whether buildings have been seismically retrofitted.
